A Tribute to the Wu-Tang Clan

The Wu-Tang Clan are a hip hop group formed in the New York City borough of Staten Island in 1992. Stylistically, they set themselves apart through their aggressive "hardcore" lyrics and "gritty" musical production style which relies heavily on samples of classic soul and R&B records as well as old Chinese Kung Fu movies. The group consists of 9 members: RZA, GZA, Old Dirty Bastard, Inspektah Deck, Raekwon, Masta Killa, U-God, Ghostface Killah, and Method Man.

Their debut album "Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers)", released in 1993, was certified platinum by 1995 and has sold over 2 million copies in the US alone.

Like Meth went with Def Jam, Rae stayed with Loud, Ghost went with Sony, GZA went with Geffen Records, feel me? - RZA

The Wu-Tang Clan made recording industry history as the first group act to negotiate a contact that allowed its participant members to simultaneously pursue solo contracts with other record labels.

  • RZA formed a side group called Tha Gravediggaz with fellow producer Prince Paul (known for his previous work with De La Soul) and released their debut album, "6 Feet Deep", in 1994 spawning the hip hop subgenre of "horrorcore".
  • Method Man released his first solo album, "Tical", in 1994 and went on to win a Grammy award for the single "You're All I Need".
  • Old Dirty Bastard issued his debut album, "Return to the 36 Chambers: The Dirty Version", in 1995. It was certified gold and nominated for a Grammy award for Best Rap Album.
  • Next was Raekwon's "Only Built 4 Cuban Linx..." which sold 130,000 copies in its first week.
  • Then GZA's certified platinum "Liquid Swords".
  • And Ghostface Killah's "Ironman" in 1996 which peaked at #2 on the Billboard charts and also certified platinum.

Meanwhile, Wu-Tang launched their own clothing line, Wu Wear, and were working on their second group effort. "Wu-Tang Forever", a sprawling double album, dropped in 1997 at #1 on the Billboard 200 chart. It was certified 4x platinum! This album spawned four subsequent group albums as well as countless more solo and affiliate albums.

In addition to the clothing label, the members have grown their brand in countless other ways. RZA has scored feature films (including "Ghost Dog: Way of the Samurai" and "Kill Bill: Vol 1"). He's also written two books, "The Wu-Tang Manual" and "The Tao of the Wu". He scored and produced the movie "The Man with the Iron Fists". He and GZA were in the film "Coffee and Cigarettes" alongside Bill Murray. Method Man's acting career has taken off landing him major roles in movies and TV series ("How High" with Redman, "Keanu", and HBO's "The Wire").

It was often rumored that there were as many as 360 members in the Wu-Tang Clan periphery. This number is likely fabricated and chosen only for the symbolism of 360 representing the degrees of a circle: a metphor for the vast sphere of influence RZA and the rest of the Clan had over the hip hop world. Though the number may not truly be quite so high, the artists who have worked with or been influenced by Wu-Tang is innumberable.

The group's financial success prompted a wildly popular bit on the sketch comedy program "Chapelle's Show" entitled "Wu-Tang Financial". Members of the Clan, RZA and GZA, were featured guests on the episode, playing the roles of advisors at their fictional financial planning company of the same name. With the motto based on their hit song "C.R.E.A.M.", the company was known to operate on this basis of "Cash Rules Everything Around Me".
